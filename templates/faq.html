Q: What are the benefits of feeding insects to my pets?

A: Insects are a natural and nutritious food source for many animals, such as reptiles, amphibians, birds, and fish. They provide essential protein, fat, vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients that your pets need for optimal health and growth. Feeding insects can also stimulate your pets’ natural hunting and foraging behaviors, enriching their lives and preventing boredom.

Q: How do I keep my feeder insects alive and healthy?

A: Keeping your feeder insects alive and healthy is important for their nutritional value and your pets’ safety. You should follow these general guidelines:

  • Store your feeder insects in a clean, well-ventilated, and appropriately sized container. Avoid overcrowding and cross-contamination.
  • Provide your feeder insects with fresh food and water daily. Remove any uneaten or spoiled food and dead insects promptly.
  • Maintain the optimal temperature and humidity for your feeder insects. Avoid extreme heat, cold, or dryness.
  • Gut load your feeder insects 24-48 hours before feeding them to your pets. This means giving them a high-quality diet that enhances their nutritional content. You can use our specially formulated gut load products for this purpose.
  • Dust your feeder insects with calcium and vitamin D3 powder before feeding them to your pets. This helps prevent metabolic bone disease and other deficiencies in your pets.

Q: How do I feed insects to my pets safely and effectively?

A: Feeding insects to your pets can be a fun and rewarding experience, but you should also take some precautions to ensure their well-being. You should follow these general tips:

  • Feed your pets the appropriate amount and frequency of insects, according to their size, age, and activity level. Overfeeding or underfeeding can cause health problems and obesity in your pets.
  • Feed your pets insects that are suitable for their mouth size and digestive system. Avoid feeding insects that are too large, too hard, or too spiny for your pets to swallow or digest. You can use our size chart to help you choose the right insects for your pets.
  • Feed your pets insects in a separate feeding container or area, away from their regular enclosure. This prevents the insects from escaping, hiding, or contaminating your pets’ habitat. It also reduces the risk of impaction, infection, or injury from substrate ingestion or insect bites.
  • Monitor your pets while they are eating insects, and remove any uneaten insects after each feeding. This prevents the insects from stressing, harming, or competing with your pets for food or space. It also helps you keep track of your pets’ appetite and health.
